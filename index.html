Principles of Earned Autonomy

A framework for safely delegating real work to AI - while remaining accountable for what gets done.

Three principles that apply to any AI model or toolset. They address the gap between AI capability and human accountability.

Two responses to this gap are common. Both break down.

"I'll just review everything." That holds for a while. Then review gets faster than actual thought, and approvals become automatic. The reviewing is still happening; the checking is not.

"The model is good enough, I'll trust the outputs." Sometimes. But "good enough" isn't a fixed property - it depends on which model, which task, which day, how the prompt was framed. You won't know which outputs were wrong until they've already been used.

The Framework

Three Principles

These are requirements. An AI setup that ignores them has a structural design problem. Each one names something the AI must never be allowed to decide on its own.

01
Commander's Intent
Define the destination. Never prescribe the route.

The problem it solves

Give an AI agent a checklist and it executes the checklist. Its ceiling is the checklist. It finds what the checklist described and misses everything else. It cannot adapt to a situation the instruction author never anticipated - and those are precisely the situations that matter most.

The alternative: the agent receives a mission - what must be achieved and why - and determines how, based on what it actually encounters. The human defines the objective. The agent discovers the method.

In practice this means giving the AI a goal - “what here doesn’t earn its existence?” - rather than a checklist of specific things to look for. It means providing vocabulary as a thinking framework, not a lookup table. The instruction defines the shape of the work. The agent finds the content.

02
Observable Autonomy
Autonomy is earned through transparency.

The problem it solves

Autonomy and transparency are usually treated as parallel concerns - independent checkboxes. They are causally linked.

Autonomy without a transparent record is abdication. Transparency requires more than a record simply existing: it must be captured as the work happens, and it must be one the agent cannot revise once the outcome is known. Otherwise the record is just a story the agent tells about itself.

More visibility builds trust, and more trust earns more autonomy. Less visibility erodes both. No visibility at all means no basis for trust.

The audit trail is how the agent earns the right to keep acting.

Roots Meaningful Human Control Trust Calibration - Lee & See, 2004
03
Convergence Is Silence
Converged when independent evaluators find nothing left to change.

The problem it solves

AI improvement cycles tend to declare completion too early. The usual test - "the score stopped going up" - measures the wrong thing. A score can plateau while the work is still unstable: each pass changes something, the number stays flat, but the output never settles.

Worse: a single evaluator will eventually align with its own blind spots. The score stabilizes while the work is still incomplete - the evaluator has stopped seeing what it can't see. An AI that evaluates its own output is rationalising.

The correct test: ask diverse, independent evaluators - each without knowing what the others found - to assess the work. When they all find nothing left to change, the work is done. A stable score matters. The test is whether independent evaluators agree.

Emergent property

Autonomous Reasoning Fidelity (ARF)

ARF is what all three principles produce together: the observable signal that the agent is reasoning, and that the reasoning is visible enough for outside observers to judge. It only shows up when all three principles hold - weaken any one of them, and it disappears.

Intellectual lineage

Standing on prior work

This framework draws from established traditions in military doctrine, lean manufacturing, philosophy, and empirical AI research. Each one contributed something the framework would be weaker without.

Conceptual traditions

Auftragstaktik - Prussian mission command ↗ Commander's Intent - US military adaptation ↗

19th century military doctrine, German General Staff

Defines subordinate authority by mission objective. Tells subordinates what and why, then trusts them to determine how. The direct origin of Commander's Intent.

Toyota Kata - Mike Rother ↗ Lean Enterprise Institute: Kaizen and the Coaching Kata ↗

McGraw-Hill, 2009

The Coaching Kata establishes a pattern where the coach defines the target condition and the learner discovers the path. Shapes how PEA frames the operator–agent relationship.

Socrates - Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy ↗ Socrates - Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy ↗

Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy · Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy

Reasoning through questions rather than assertions - the instruction asks, the agent arrives at the answer from its own situated reasoning.

Kaizen - Lean Enterprise Institute ↗ Kaizen - Wikipedia ↗

Toyota Production System · Lean Enterprise Institute

The natural exit condition of a kaizen loop: when diverse participants find nothing left to change. PEA formalises this as Convergence Is Silence and makes the independence condition explicit.

Delphi Method - RAND Corporation (originators) ↗ Delphi Method - Wikipedia ↗

RAND Corporation, 1950s

Structured convergence through independent expert rounds without shared context. PEA borrows the independence condition: evaluators must not share context when assessing convergence.

Empirical basis

Three peer-reviewed studies establish why behavioral alignment alone cannot substitute for structural constraints - and why an audit trail the agent authors itself is insufficient.

Language Models Don't Always Say What They Think ↗

Turpin et al. - NeurIPS 2023

CoT explanations can be plausible and systematically misleading at the same time. Stated reasoning diverges from internal reasoning - sometimes by as much as 36 percentage points on benchmark tasks.

Large Language Models Cannot Self-Correct Reasoning Yet ↗

Huang et al. - ICLR 2024

Without external feedback, LLM self-correction degrades performance rather than improving it. An agent that assesses its own work is rationalising.

Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think ↗

Chen et al. - arXiv:2505.05410, May 2025

Even reasoning-trained models do not reliably narrate their internal process. Chain-of-thought monitoring is not sufficient to rule out undesired behavior in capable models.

The Implementation

Six Skills

These six skills put the three principles into practice. Together they give any AI agent a persistent record of decisions, context, and history - so each session builds on the last.

The agent interpreted the prompt literally and missed the intent
Intent

Forces the agent to explicitly state its interpretation of the task before acting. Named after Commander's Intent - Auftragstaktik - the doctrine of defining the mission objective so subordinates can adapt when conditions change. An early warning system for misaligned assumptions.

The agent doesn't know your vision - because it lives in your head
Vision

Surfaces the agent’s working assumptions about where the operator is heading - as specific questions the operator can confirm, correct, or reject. Closes the gap between what has been written down and what the operator actually means. Produces .trail/vision.md.

The work is unauditable
Trail

Enforces Observable Autonomy. Every decision, rationale, and discarded alternative is appended to .trail/audit-trail.md as the work happens. Work that is not logged cannot be audited.

The agent makes superficial, undisciplined edits
Improve

The workhorse of the suite. Each iteration: read all prior context and history, examine the target, challenge the first instinct, make exactly one highest-leverage change, predict the outcome, verify it, reflect. Reads vision and trail before every run - never wastes an iteration on something already tried.

The agent can’t see patterns across its own work
Retrospect

Steps back and reads the entire work history as a single document: what direction is the work heading, where has attention been concentrated, is that actually where the weight lies? Surfaces what no individual session would reveal. Produces .trail/retrospect.md.

You can't tell if the agent is reasoning or pattern-matching
Probe

Constructs a novelty probe - a pair of cases that look similar but differ in a material way - to test whether the agent's response diverges where it should. Measures Autonomous Reasoning Fidelity directly. Used for validation runs.

Memory Model

The .trail/ folder is the memory

An agent without memory repeats yesterday's mistakes and forgets yesterday's decisions. The skills suite gives every repository a memory layer that lives in .trail/ at the repo root. Each Improve run reads it before acting and extends it after. This is what makes the loop cumulative instead of amnesiac. 

.trail/              # lives in the root of the repo being worked on
├── vision.md          # the destination (operator-held, never written by skills)
├── retrospect.md      # current orientation + active operational rules (Retrospect rewrites each run)
├── learning.md        # derived: every [!REALIZATION] and [!REVERSAL] across the trail (regenerated by Trail)
├── history.md         # derived: run timeline (regenerated by Trail at every commit)
├── audit-trail.md     # append-only source of truth - one entry per session
├── sessions/          # mandatory: per-session summary file, one per run, linked from audit-trail.md
│   └── YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md
└── transcripts/       # optional: verbatim transcript exports when the harness supports it
    └── YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md

Three tiers. Operator layer: vision.md - never written by any skill. Synthesis layer: retrospect.md, learning.md, history.md - rewritten or regenerated each run, all derived from the evidence below. Evidence layer: audit-trail.md, sessions/, transcripts/ - append-only, never edited after the fact.

vision.md
Where you're going. The operator-held destination. What the target is for, the constraints that hold across all runs, the hard problem the work is chipping at. Stable. No skill ever writes to it - only humans do.
retrospect.md
Where you are. The current arc-level orientation derived from reading the whole trail as one document. Contains the active operational rules the agent must obey this run. Rewritten each time the Retrospect skill runs.
learning.md
What you've learned. A generated, chronological extract of every [!REALIZATION] and [!REVERSAL] marker in the trail. The compact form of the loop's accumulated insight - read this before reaching for the full history. Regenerated by Trail at every commit so it never lags behind.
history.md
The timeline. A generated run-by-run index derived from audit-trail.md. Faster to scan than the full evidence layer when you just need to know what happened when. Regenerated by Trail at every commit.
audit-trail.md
The path you walked. Append-only ledger. Every session writes one entry containing its interpretation of the ask, its examination, decisions, actions, and reflection. This is the source of truth - the synthesis layer above is derived from it.
sessions/
The full summaries. Mandatory. One file per run (YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md), linked from the matching audit-trail.md entry. The audit ledger is the short-form index; the session file is the long-form summary. Structural distinction is required - one file cannot play both roles.
transcripts/
The verbatim record. Optional, harness-dependent. When the platform can export the raw operator/agent dialogue, it lands here as YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>.md. Highest-trust evidence tier when available - no reconstruction, no paraphrase.

The hierarchy resolves disagreements: if vision.md and retrospect.md disagree, vision wins (the operator holds the destination). If retrospect.md and audit-trail.md disagree, the trail wins (the trail is the evidence). Memory without a hierarchy is just noise.

Interpretation of the ask

Run the full Improve loop on the repo using the operator's convergence-loop prompt as the initial brief. Intent: find the highest-leverage remaining change that makes the skills more effective on any arbitrary codebase, or declare convergence if nothing remains. Constraints: generic first, no test infrastructure, human-readable, one change per run.

Examination

Inconsistency lens. The opening paragraph read "It stopped when three independent evaluators from distinct model families found nothing left to change." The Evidence section reads "That is the convergence baseline at v3.1.0 (2026-04-24)" and the Reference section shows the project at v3.6.0 - six releases after the claimed "stop." The word "stopped" implies permanent cessation. PRINCIPLES.md defines convergence as evaluators finding nothing to change at a point in time, not as the project ending. The opening misstated what convergence means. The Evidence section used the correct framing ("convergence baseline"); the opening did not.

Challenge. Considered whether a structural gap in Probe's connection to the convergence protocol was higher leverage - but Probe already states "convergence (Principle 3) is the meta-measurement." Considered whether first-run / no-trail clarity in Intent was a real gap - but Trail's initialization instruction and any competent agent bridges it. The "stopped" inconsistency is the one place where the opening actively misstates the core concept the entire suite is built on. That is the highest leverage finding.

Decision

[!DECISION] Change "stopped" to "converged" in the opening paragraph. One word. The opening is the first statement any reader or agent encounters about what the loop does. If it encodes the wrong mental model of convergence (permanent cessation vs. point-in-time state), that model propagates into how the agent understands the stopping condition. Alternatives ranked: (1) this change - highest leverage per word changed; (2) add first-run guidance to Intent - addresses a minor edge case; (3) declare convergence - premature given the identified inconsistency.

Action

Changed "It stopped when three independent evaluators" to "It converged when three independent evaluators" in the opening paragraph of README.md. verify.py passes after change.

Reflection

The loop is converging. This session has been primarily README editorial work - the skills themselves are unchanged. The remaining gap is whether a new convergence run (with a different model family) agrees. This run's finding was small but precise: one word that misstated the central concept. That is what late-stage convergence looks like - the large structural changes happened in prior sessions; what remains is semantic precision.
