Safely delegate complex work to AI and stay accountable.

Six skills that make an AI's reasoning visible and auditable - so you can delegate the work and still own the result.

Principles of Earned Autonomy (PEA)

Principle #1: Commander’s Intent ↗

“Define the destination. Never prescribe the route.”

Instructions are a cage; intent is a destination. Prescribe every step and the AI can only obey - it cannot reason, adapt, or catch the mistake you did not foresee. Give it the what and the why, and one move buys both: more autonomy, because it reasons its own route, and more safety, because it is aimed at your goal - not a literal misreading of it.

Rooted in: Auftragstaktik, Toyota Coaching Kata, Socratic Method

Principle #2: Observable Autonomy ↗

“The degree of autonomy a system deserves is bounded by the degree of transparency it provides.”

All autonomous actions must be auditable or revoked.

Rooted in: Saltzer & Schroeder, Flight Data Recorder, Reproducibility, Turpin et al. ↗, Huang et al. ↗, Chen et al. ↗

Principle #3: Convergence Is Silence ↗

“Not done until multiple independent models find nothing to change.”

Definition of when the improve loop has achieved the desired outcome.

Rooted in: Kaizen, Delphi Method, Reproducibility

Autonomous Reasoning Fidelity (ARF) ↗

Appears only when all three principles hold.

ARF is the signal that measures if the agent is reasoning visibly enough to earn autonomy (Principles of Earned Autonomy). Probe is the skill that tests for it.

The Skills

Install

Download the six skill files from the skills repository and place them in your agent's skills folder (.copilot/skills/ for GitHub Copilot, or wherever your toolset reads custom instructions from).

Go to the skills repository ↗

Intent SKILL ↗

Force the Agent to understand the intent behind your prompt in relation to the context: what you mean, not what you said. Cites how it reads your intent before it continues.

The principle behind military command, Socratic method, and coaching kata.

To trigger: write “understand my intent” every prompt.

Derives from: P1 · Commander’s Intent, Auftragstaktik

Trail SKILL ↗

Writes every session to .trail/audit-trail.md - the append-only source of truth. One entry per run: the ask, examination, decisions, actions, reflection. The next prompt knows what was already done and why.

From that record it keeps readable views in sync at each commit - a run timeline and a log of every realization and reversal - so you never have to reconstruct the history by hand.

To trigger: write “trail everything” every session.

Derives from: P2 · Observable Autonomy, Flight Data Recorder, Reproducibility, Huang et al. ↗

Improve SKILL ↗

Iterative improvement loop. Improves any target by understanding the context, purpose and intent. Applied to itself 200+ times.

One highest leverage change per run. Reads the context first - destination, retrospect, trail - acts, and logs it.

To trigger: write “use the improve skill” until .trail/destination.md is fulfilled.

Derives from: P3 · Convergence Is Silence, Toyota Coaching Kata, Kaizen

Destination SKILL ↗

The agent reads the conversation and the trail and states what it thinks you're building and why, as specific questions for you to confirm or correct. Produces .trail/destination.md - where you're going: what the target is for, the constraints that hold across runs, the hard problem the work is chipping at. Operator-held; humans only.

To trigger: write “use the destination skill” at the start, when direction feels unclear.

Derives from: P1 · Commander’s Intent

Retrospect SKILL ↗

Reads the entire trail. Self-reflects on all iterations and surfaces patterns no single session can see. Produces .trail/retrospect.md - where you are: arc-level orientation plus the active rules the agent must obey this run.

To trigger: write “use the retro skill” after a few Improve iterations.

Derives from: P2 · Observable Autonomy, P3 · Convergence Is Silence

Probe SKILL ↗

Tests whether the agent’s response diverges where it should. Measures Autonomous Reasoning Fidelity.

To trigger: write “use the probe skill” when you need to verify reasoning quality.

Derives from: Socratic Method, Turpin et al. ↗, Chen et al. ↗

Memory Model

The skills give every repository a memory layer in .trail/, written to the root of the repo being worked on. Each run reads it before acting and extends it after - that is what makes the loop cumulative instead of repeating yesterday's mistakes.

When two of them disagree, a fixed order settles it: your destination.md outranks the agent's read of where things stand in retrospect.md, and the append-only audit-trail.md outranks both - evidence beats opinion. Memory without a hierarchy is just noise.

Built by the loop it describes

Without data, you're just another person with an opinion. attributed to W. Edwards Deming

The skillset improves any target by understanding its purpose - it was aimed at itself. 200+ times, with different AI models, each pass improving its own ability, until no model could improve it further. It began as Kaizen, borrowed from Toyota Kata. It split into Kaizen, Kaikaku, and Hansei. It was unified into the single Improve skill in use today. Each version was sharpened by the version before it.

Every run did exactly what this page describes: read the trail, understand the purpose of its target, make one highest-leverage change, and record the reasoning. The target just happened to be the loop itself - an improvement process with enough awareness to examine and rewrite its own definition while understanding what that definition was for.

It stopped the only way Convergence Is Silence allows: the loop is done when independent evaluators find nothing left to change. This skillset reached that point when multiple AI models, from different families, each ran the loop and each found nothing to improve while the principles still held. The skillset is not a design anyone argued for - it is the fixed point the loop converged on.

Intellectual lineage

Military doctrine, lean manufacturing, philosophy, the scientific method, aviation safety, and empirical AI research.

Conceptual traditions

Auftragstaktik (Mission Command)

Subordinates are told what and why - never how. The direct origin of Commander's Intent.

19th century military doctrine, German General Staff

Auftragstaktik - mission command doctrine (Wikipedia) ↗ Auftragstaktik - Military Review (US Army Combined Arms Center, 2002, PDF) ↗ Commander's Intent (Wikipedia) ↗

Applies in: Principle 1 - Commander's Intent, Intent skill

Toyota Coaching Kata

The coach defines the target condition. The learner discovers the path. The same shape as the operator-agent relationship.

Mike Rother, Toyota Kata · McGraw-Hill, 2009

Toyota Kata - the Improvement Kata (Wikipedia) ↗ Mike Rother, Toyota Kata (2009) ↗ Lean Enterprise Institute - the Coaching Kata ↗

Applies in: Principle 1 - Commander's Intent, Improve skill

Socratic Method

Reasoning through questions, not assertions. The instruction asks; the agent answers from its own situated reasoning.

Socratic method · classical antiquity

Socrates - Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy ↗ The Socratic Method (the elenchus) - Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy ↗

Applies in: Principle 1 - Commander's Intent, Probe skill

Saltzer & Schroeder - Separation of Privilege (1975)

A mechanism requiring two independent parties is more robust than one that trusts a single key-holder. Observable Autonomy transfers this structure from access control to the epistemic record: the party that acts must not also be the sole author of the account of its action - no single act of self-narration should be sufficient to compromise the integrity of the trail. The principle is theirs; the transfer to an autonomous agent's reasoning record is what PEA adds.

Jerome H. Saltzer & Michael D. Schroeder · MIT, Proceedings of the IEEE 63(9), 1975

The Protection of Information in Computer Systems - MIT (Saltzer) ↗ The Protection of Information in Computer Systems - original paper, p.9 (Cambridge PDF) ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy

Flight Data Recorder (the “black box”)

Record everything to a crash-survivable, tamper-resistant device, so that after any incident the full history can be independently reconstructed. The system is trusted because its actions are reviewable - not because they were assumed correct.

David Warren, ARL Australia, 1954 · mandatory in commercial aircraft since 1967

Flight recorder - recording for accident investigation (Wikipedia) ↗ David Warren - “A Device for Assisting Investigation into Aircraft Accidents” (Wikipedia) ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy, Trail skill

Reproducibility (the scientific method)

A result counts as knowledge only after independent parties can re-derive it from a transparent record. Reasoning that cannot be reproduced and checked is not yet trustworthy - the same standard PEA holds an agent's work to.

Robert Boyle, 17th century · Karl Popper, The Logic of Scientific Discovery, 1934

Reproducibility - a principle of the scientific method (Wikipedia) ↗ Reproducible research - a transparent, reviewable record (Wikipedia) ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy, Principle 3 - Convergence Is Silence, Trail skill

Kaizen

The natural exit condition of a kaizen loop: when diverse participants find nothing left to change. PEA formalises this as Convergence Is Silence and makes the independence condition explicit.

Toyota Production System · Lean Enterprise Institute

Kaizen - Lean Enterprise Institute ↗ Kaizen - continuous improvement (Wikipedia) ↗ Masaaki Imai, Kaizen (1986) ↗

Applies in: Principle 3 - Convergence Is Silence, Improve skill

Delphi Method

Convergence by independent expert rounds, no shared context. PEA's independence condition comes from here.

RAND Corporation, 1950s

Delphi Method - RAND Corporation (originators) ↗ Dalkey & Helmer, the original Delphi experiment (1963) ↗ Delphi Method - independence & anonymity (Wikipedia) ↗

Applies in: Principle 3 - Convergence Is Silence

Empirical basis

Three peer-reviewed studies establish why behavioral alignment alone cannot substitute for structural constraints, and why an audit trail the agent authors itself is insufficient.

CoT explanations can be plausible and systematically misleading at the same time. Stated reasoning diverges from internal reasoning - sometimes by as much as 36 percentage points on benchmark tasks.

Turpin et al. · NeurIPS 2023

Language Models Don't Always Say What They Think ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy, Probe skill

Without external feedback, LLM self-correction degrades performance rather than improving it. An agent that assesses its own work is rationalising.

Huang et al. · ICLR 2024

Large Language Models Cannot Self-Correct Reasoning Yet ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy, Trail skill

Even reasoning-trained models do not reliably narrate their internal process. Chain-of-thought monitoring cannot rule out undesired behavior in capable models.

Chen et al. · arXiv:2505.05410, May 2025

Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think ↗

Applies in: Principle 2 - Observable Autonomy, Probe skill

On authorship

These principles were not invented here. Auftragstaktik is Prussian military doctrine from the 1870s. The Socratic Method is ancient Greek philosophy. The Delphi Method is from RAND in the 1950s. Kaizen is post-war Japanese manufacturing. Separation of privilege is a 1975 computer security principle. The empirical evidence is peer-reviewed AI research from 2023-2025.

The contribution is the synthesis: recognizing that these traditions, combined, answer a specific problem that did not exist when any of them were written - how to delegate real work to an autonomous AI agent and remain structurally accountable for the result. No individual principle is new. The problem they jointly solve is.

The citations throughout this page are to primary sources. They predate every AI governance framework published since 2020, including this one.
